### **Problem Statement**

Investors and analysts need to understand and anticipate stock price movements to make informed financial decisions. Apple Inc., being one of the most traded and influential stocks, exhibits trends, volatility, and patterns over time that are challenging to interpret without proper analysis. There is a need for a systematic approach to **analyze historical stock data and forecast future prices**, enabling better decision-making and strategy formulation.

### **Objective**

* To **analyze historical Apple stock price data** from 2015 onwards.
* To **explore trends, patterns, and volatility** in stock prices.
* To **forecast future stock prices** using time series analysis techniques.
* To create a **clear, visual, and non-technical report** that communicates insights effectively for investors and stakeholders.

2.dataset summary

* **Dataset Source:** Historical Apple (AAPL) stock prices
* **Time Period:** January 2, 2015 – December 31 2024
* **Total Rows:** 2515 (each row represents a trading day)
* **Total Columns:** 5

  * **Close:** Closing stock price (float)
  * **High:** Highest price of the day (float)
  * **Low:** Lowest price of the day (float)
  * **Open:** Opening stock price (float)
  * **Volume:** Number of shares traded (int)
* **Index:** `Date` (DatetimeIndex), chronological order

* **Missing Data:** No missing trading days, but weekends and market holidays are naturally excluded
* **Column Types Count:**

  * `float64`: 4 columns (Close, High, Low, Open)
  * `int64`: 1 column (Volume)

## **Time Series EDA Workflow**

1. **Understand Data**

   * Check rows, columns, data types
   * Set time column as index

2. **Missing / Duplicate Timestamps**

   * Ensure chronological order
   * Handle missing days / duplicates

3. **Descriptive Statistics**

   * Summary: mean, min, max, std
   * Detect outliers

4. **Visualization**

   * Plot trends: Close, Open, High, Low, Volume
   * Rolling mean & std for trend & volatility
   * Observe seasonality patterns

5. **Decomposition**

   * Split series into Trend, Seasonality, Residual

6. **Stationarity Check**

   * ADF / KPSS tests
   * Check rolling mean & variance

7. **Correlation & Autocorrelation**

   * Plot ACF / PACF
   * Identify lags for modeling

8. **Feature Engineering**

   * Extract day, month, quarter
   * Create lag or rolling features
